Tribute to Earth Planetarians

O mighty Earth, where life takes root,

In hues of green and skies so blue.

From mountain peaks to oceans deep,

Your beauty wakes from ancient sleep.


Cradle forests, vast and wide,

Where whispering winds and rivers glide.

Your valleys bloom with flowers bright,

A canvas painted by morning light.


Deserts glow with golden sands,

The seas caress your gentle lands.

From frozen poles to tropic shores,

You are the breath that life adores.


Your skies, all creatures play,

You gift them night, you gift them day.

Your pulse is felt in every tree,

A heartbeat shared by all that be.


We, your children, often blind,

Forget your grace, your gift so kind.

We carve and take, we build and burn,

Yet still, for us, your rivers turn.


Precious Earth, so strong, so frail,

We owe you more than words can tell.

May we protect, may we defend,

Our only home, our dearest friend.
